# Write the Docs — Strategy Brief

Pre-read for the board offsite. A discussion input, not an approved plan.

## The situation

AI is reshaping documentation, and the evidence points to opportunity, not decline:

- US tech-writing employment growth has been revised down to ~1% for 2024–2034,
  attributed to AI productivity — not job loss. The role is shifting toward
  curation, structure, and quality.
- AI is now a primary *consumer* of docs (Gartner expects ~70% of firms running
  retrieval bots by 2026), so documentation quality matters more, not less.
- Our own community is already working through this in Slack and the newsletter.

WTD holds rare assets — a vendor-neutral brand, a content canon (Guide, the
~90-issue newsletter, the Topics graph, a decade of talks), the salary survey,
and global events. They're under-leveraged and run entirely on volunteer effort.

## The choice

Does WTD step up as the place the profession turns to as AI reshapes the craft,
and resource it accordingly — or stay a beloved but lightly-resourced community?

## Proposed focus

Two priorities; we can't do everything on volunteer time.

1. **Turn our content into durable, citable infrastructure.** A
   searchable/transcribed talk archive, an automatically maintained Topics graph,
   and structured content that AI answers cite. Mostly automatable.
2. **Lead the conversation on AI and documentation.** A standing content theme
   and conference programming, vendor-neutral.

Sustainability sits under both: automation cuts volunteer load, and a stronger
sponsor story (plus a possible supporter tier) reduces funding concentration.

## Decisions for the offsite

1. **Focus** — confirm the two priorities, or pick others.
2. **AI policy** — propose a simple stance: AI may assist, a human signs off, we
   disclose it.
3. **Editorial** — grow the newsletter into a lightly curated publication, or
   hold steady?
4. **Funding** — diversify (supporter tier, grants), or not?
5. **Translation** — enable community-led locales, or decline clearly? (Our
   most-requested, repeatedly-deferred ask.)
6. **Measurement** — adopt a small shared scorecard?

## Where to start

Low-regret and mostly AI-built — useful under any strategy:

- Fix funnel leaks: monitor the Slack invite, check external links, add social
  preview images.
- Accessibility and SEO basics: alt text, site-wide metadata, fix the 404 links.
- Prototype the talk archive: transcribe and tag a few talks to prove the pattern.
