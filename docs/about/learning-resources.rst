Over the years, our community has produced a deep library of talks, articles, and guides.
Here's where to start.

Documentation Guide
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The :doc:`/guide/index` is a best-practice handbook for building, structuring, and writing software documentation, for both novice and expert writers. It's our most comprehensive resource.

Topic Index
~~~~~~~~~~~

The :doc:`/topics` is a curated archive of every conference talk and newsletter article we've published, organized by subject. If you're looking for material on a specific topic in documentation, start here.

Talks and videos
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

All of our conference talks are available on the `Write the Docs YouTube channel <https://www.youtube.com/c/WritetheDocs>`_, so you can catch up on the content even if you couldn't attend the conferences in person.

Podcast
~~~~~~~

The :doc:`/podcast` is brought to you by our community members and highlights some of the discussions and stories that happen at our various events and online spaces.

Guide to Hiring and Getting Hired
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The :doc:`/hiring-guide/index` is a growing set of resources for those who want to hire or be hired as a documentarian. It covers interviewing, portfolios, useful skills, and freelancing.

Salary Surveys
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

We periodically run `salary surveys </surveys/>`_ to help our community understand industry pay. The most recent results are the `Write the Docs Salary Survey 2022 Results </surveys/salary-survey/2022/>`_.

Book Club Write-ups
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The #learn-tech-writing channel in the Write the Docs :doc:`/slack` hosts a book club. The :doc:`/book-club/index` contains summaries of their discussions.

Reading List
~~~~~~~~~~~~

Our :doc:`/books` page is a curated list of books related to technical writing, information architecture, and content strategy.

Organizer Guide
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The :doc:`/organizer-guide/index` is a set of resources for folks who want to organize a Write the Docs meetup, conference, or other event.
