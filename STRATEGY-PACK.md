## Strategy on a page

**The moment.** AI is reshaping documentation. Tech-writing growth has cooled
(BLS ~1% for 2024–2034, blamed on AI productivity, not job loss), AI is now a
primary consumer of docs (~70% of firms running RAG bots by 2026), and EU AI
literacy is law as of Feb 2025. The role is shifting to curation, architecture,
and quality — and good docs matter *more*. WTD's own community is already asking
the field's hardest AI questions.

**The asset.** A vendor-neutral brand; a content canon (Guide + 94-issue
newsletter + Topics knowledge graph + 10 years of talks); a unique salary survey;
global events (Portland, European, Australia, Kenya) plus meetups, Slack, podcast.
Most of it is **under-leveraged** and **run on manual volunteer effort.**

**The central question.** Does WTD step up to be the place the profession turns to
as AI reshapes the craft — and resource it — or stay a beloved, lightly-resourced
community?

**Four pillars (can't fund all at once):**
- **A — Lead the profession through the AI transition.** Be the trusted convener.
- **B — Turn our content into lasting, citable infrastructure.** Searchable talk
  archive, maintained Topics graph, structured/citable content (not `llms.txt`).
- **C — Make the community sustainable.** Automate manual chores; diversify
  funding; build volunteer succession.
- **D — Grow access and reach.** Global expansion, opportunity grants,
  accessibility, adjacent roles.

**Six decisions for the offsite:** (1) which two pillars to focus on; (2) our AI
posture + policy on AI-assisted content; (3) editorial ambition beyond the
newsletter; (4) funding model (membership/grants?); (5) internationalization
stance; (6) a shared success scorecard.

**The throughline.** WTD's binding constraint is volunteer attention. The winning
plays let **AI/automation do the production work while humans keep the judgment**
— creating capacity rather than consuming it.

## How AI can help execute (cross-cutting)

Once a direction is chosen, much of the work is AI-implementable with human
sign-off — e.g. transcribing/tagging the talk archive, repurposing the newsletter
archive, maintaining the Topics graph, generating social/OG cards, fixing
accessibility/SEO, and drafting sponsor/marketing assets. Details and priorities
are in the two reports.
