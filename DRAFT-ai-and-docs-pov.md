## Why we're saying something

Our community keeps asking the same questions, in Slack and in our own newsletter:
*Does AI mean people don't need the docs? Is my role safe? How do I prove
documentation's value?* Write the Docs has always been the place documentarians
work these things out together. On the biggest shift our craft has seen in a
generation, silence isn't neutral — it cedes the conversation to vendors selling
the anxiety. Here is where we stand.

## What we believe

**1. AI raises the value of good documentation — it doesn't retire it.**
AI systems are now a primary *reader* of documentation. Retrieval-augmented
assistants, code copilots, and support bots are only as good as the docs they
stand on. Garbage in, confident garbage out. The clearer and more accurate our
documentation, the better it serves humans *and* machines. Quality matters more
now, not less.

**2. The role is shifting, not disappearing.**
The value of a documentarian is moving from producing words to *curating,
structuring, fact-checking, and owning quality* — being the knowledge architect
who decides what is true, what matters, and how it fits together. AI can draft;
it can't be accountable. Notably, AI tends to **enlarge the maintenance surface**
— more content, faster, decaying faster — which makes skilled stewards more
necessary, not less.

**3. Write for humans first; the machines benefit too.**
There's pressure to write "for the LLM" — to churn out FAQs and keyword soup.
Resist it. Documentation that is genuinely clear, well-structured, and honest is
exactly what AI extracts and cites most reliably. Good writing for people is good
writing for everything.

**4. Use AI deliberately, transparently, and on your own terms.**
AI is a tool documentarians can use to do better work — drafting, summarizing,
translating, tagging — when it adds value and doesn't compromise quality. Use it
where it helps; disclose it where it matters; keep a human accountable for what
ships. (We hold ourselves to this: see our AI-assisted-content policy.)

**5. Prove the value — now you can.**
The same AI wave gives us better tools to *measure* documentation's impact:
ticket deflection, search success, contributor activity, real user outcomes. Turn
the usually-invisible value of docs into numbers leadership understands. The best
defense against "can't AI just do this?" is evidence of what good documentation
is worth.

## What Write the Docs will do

- **Convene the conversation** — talks, newsletter, and Slack as the place the
  profession reasons about AI and docs, vendor-neutrally.
- **Share what works** — curate practical guidance on using AI well, proving
  value, and maintaining quality at AI speed.
- **Keep our canon trustworthy** — invest in the Guide, the newsletter archive,
  and the talk library as a durable, citable, human-grounded reference.
- **Stay human** — our community's voice, judgment, and care are the point. AI
  helps us scale them; it doesn't replace them.
