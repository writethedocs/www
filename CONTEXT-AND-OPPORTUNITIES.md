# Write the Docs — Context & Opportunities

Companion to `STRATEGY.md`. Part 1 is the external data behind the strategy;
Part 2 is where the opportunity sits, prioritized by impact. Kept terse on
purpose.

Figures are directional (varied sources, mid-2026); sources at the end.

---

## Part 1 — The data

### AI and the profession
| Signal | Figure | So what |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tech-writing job growth | ~1% for 2024–34, down from 7% (BLS) — blamed on AI productivity, *not* job loss | The role shifts to curation, structure, quality |
| AI literacy | Legal duty in the EU since Feb 2025 (AI Act Art. 4) | Real demand for credible, vendor-neutral AI+docs education |
| Maintenance | AI speeds drafting but enlarges the maintenance surface | A structural argument *for* skilled documentarians |

### How docs are consumed now
| Signal | Figure | So what |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Retrieval (RAG) bots | ~70% of companies by 2026 (Gartner) | AI is now a primary *reader* of docs |
| Support deflection | 40–50% of routine tickets — only on accurate docs | Quality matters *more*, not less |
| Community view | "AI is a new audience for documentation" (our own newsletter) | WTD can own: good docs are the AI era's foundation |

### Content authority (GEO)
| Signal | Figure | So what |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AI-referred traffic | ~527% YoY growth, early 2025 | Being *cited by AI* is the new visibility |
| What gets cited | Self-contained, answer-first, fact-dense, schema-marked content | Describes good documentation almost exactly |
| `llms.txt` | No major AI crawler uses it; Google rejected it | Don't bet on it — bet on clean, citable, structured content |

### Events & funding
| Signal | Figure | So what |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Event formats | In-person ~60% of revenue; ~75% of planners now run hybrid | WTD's in-person + virtual + global model is well-aligned |
| Funding resilience | Diversified income + tiered membership materially more sustainable | Sponsor+ticket concentration is a risk |
| Engagement → money | Engaged communities raise ~60% more | Engagement and funding are one problem, not two |

## Part 2 — Where the opportunity is

WTD's leverage is its **under-used assets** (Guide, ~90-issue newsletter, Topics
graph, a decade of talks, the salary survey) — most run on manual volunteer
effort. Ranked by impact within each area.

### Content & authority — highest leverage
| Opportunity | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Make the talk archive searchable (transcripts, summaries, tags) | A decade of talks, currently near-undiscoverable; SEO + accessibility + reuse |
| Amplify the Slack→Newsletter→Topics engine | ~90 issues of evergreen content, under-distributed; auto-maintain the Topics graph (#1332, #1298, #952) |
| Make the canon citable (GEO) | Structured, schema-marked content so AI answers cite WTD |
| Interactive salary survey | Unique data, currently static prose (#2102) |

### Community & conferences
| Opportunity | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Fix funnel leaks | Expired Slack invites (#2419, #2250, #2238) and missing social-preview images (#2704) leak growth daily |
| Strengthen sponsor value | Audience one-pager + post-conf recaps → renewals; reduces funding risk |
| Use the newsletter harder | Already on Mailchimp with segments; retention backbone, under-leveraged |
| "Convince your manager" + testimonials | Long-requested conversion asset (#419) |

### Site & foundations
| Opportunity | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| External link checking | CI skips external links → recurring dead links (#1079, #1334, #1734, #2009) |
| Accessibility & SEO basics | Alt text not enforced; main pages lack social metadata; add event schema (#1026) |
| Automate manual config | Sponsors-when-present (#2053), auto-link current conference (#1680) |
| 404 page links | Logo/breadcrumb point to `#`, trapping lost visitors (#2033) |
